Fitting 12 pieces of gum into a box never looked so comfortable. fields: Packaging, branding, visual identity, motion graphics, marketing roles: Sketch, design, print challenge: A second year college study. An aluminum pack wrapping each piece in plastic is annoying to use. It is a small step but nonetheless one to take into consideration in order for customers to get the most of their purchase. The blister pack can be compared to a loud bag of chips rustling around or the look of a sick person’s medication. When the pieces are punched out, it creates a messy display of aluminum and squished plastic which does not reflect Excel’s image well. goal: Create convenient, silent & modern packaging that reduces the amount of trash in landfills. process1: To begin, I conducted market research. This gave me the opportunties to gain insight on what other competition had on the market. Researching the target audience gave me a feel for who I was designing for. process2: While sketching, I had to stay true to the purpose of efficiency and a minimal amount of materials used. finalproduct1: The redesign completely removes the need for an aluminum pack, featuring a completely biodegradable paper box. Energy usage in production is lessened without the need to produce the aluminum and plastic blister pack. While aluminum is sustainable in the sense that it can be reused, it can still contaminate because not all recycling centres accept it. Taking about 400 years to break down in the landfills, it is nothing compared to the few months it takes for paper. finalproduct2: Excel’s young adult and adult target market will appreciate the improved look and compact feel that was taken in the creative direction. A new look is a must if Excel wishes to display their company values and exhibit the emotional benefit of chewing their gum. ---
